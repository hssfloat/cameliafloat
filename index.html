✦ Ancient Roots

A Festival Born
from Legend & Love

Holi is one of the oldest and most beloved Hindu festivals, with roots stretching back thousands of years across the Indian subcontinent. Its origins weave together mythology, devotion, and the eternal cycle of seasons.


The most celebrated legend tells of Prahlad, a young devotee of Lord Vishnu, and his evil aunt Holika — immune to fire — who tried to burn him alive. Through divine grace, Prahlad emerged unharmed while Holika perished. That bonfire, called Holika Dahan, is lit the night before Holi across India to this day, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.


Another beloved story comes from the playful young Krishna, who smeared colored powders on his beloved Radha and the gopis of Vrindavan — a tradition that evolved into the riotous, joyful color-throwing we know and love today. In Vrindavan and Mathura, Holi celebrations last a full week, drawing pilgrims from around the world.


Holi also marks the arrival of spring, celebrating the end of winter's cold grip and the blossoming of life, love, and abundance. It's a time when social barriers dissolve — caste, class, and age all fade as everyone stands equal under a rainbow of color.

Colors Everywhere

Powdered pigments in magenta, turmeric yellow, sky blue, and vibrant green fill the air — you throw it, wear it, breathe it. No one escapes!

💃

Music & Dance

Dhol drumbeats echo through the streets. Bollywood bangers, folk songs, and bhangra rhythms keep the party alive from dawn to dusk.

🍬

Sweets & Thandai

Gujiya (stuffed pastries), malpua, and the iconic rose-and-saffron thandai milk drink make every celebration a delicious feast for the senses.

🤝

No Barriers

On Holi, everyone is equal. Strangers become friends, old grudges dissolve, and communities reunite under a shared explosion of color and laughter.

💦

Water Fights

Pichkaris (water guns) and water balloons filled with colored water add a soaking-wet dimension to the powder chaos — especially delightful in LA heat!

🌸

Spring Renewal

Holi is spring's own festival. After months of cold and grey, the explosion of color is nature herself celebrating — and inviting you to join in.

✦ Southern California

Holi Comes Alive
in Los Angeles

Los Angeles — home to one of the largest South Asian communities in North America — celebrates Holi with spectacular energy. From beachside color runs to temple celebrations in the San Gabriel Valley, the city offers some of the most vibrant Holi events outside of India.

📍 Artesia & Little India, Cerritos

Little India Holi Mela

The heart of LA's South Asian community erupts each March with street celebrations, authentic Indian food, live bhangra performances, and massive communal color throwing along Pioneer Blvd.

📍 Dockweiler Beach / Venice

Holi & the Beach

A beloved annual gathering where thousands converge at the ocean for color powder festivities with the Pacific as backdrop — a uniquely Californian Holi experience that draws revelers from all over SoCal.

📍 ISKCON Temple, Culver City

ISKCON Festival of Colors

The Hare Krishna temple hosts a deeply spiritual yet joyful Holi, where kirtan (devotional music), free prasad (blessed food), and organic color powder create an unforgettable community celebration.

📍 San Gabriel Valley

Community Block Parties

Across Arcadia, Rowland Heights, Diamond Bar, and Temple City, neighborhood organizations and cultural associations host intimate block-level Holi parties that keep local traditions alive and accessible.
